The Heat — in 90 seconds.

Diary of a warming planet. An automated climate desk that surfaces events extraordinary enough to deserve a stranger’s attention, paired with one calm sentence explaining the system behind the number. Use this guide when you’re about to post, design a card, or pick a color.

LOCKUP01

Mark + wordmark

  • Wordmark. Inter SemiBold, tracking −0.022em. Capital T & H, lowercase otherwise. Never all-caps, never italic.
  • Mark. Thermometer with accent bulb. Mark height = wordmark cap-height × 1.6. Gap between mark and wordmark = wordmark cap-height × 0.55.
  • Clear space. Equal to bulb diameter on every side.
  • Min size. Lockup ≥ 80px wide. Below that, mark only.

  • Accent goes on the integer. The whole point of the page. Not on labels, not on chrome.
  • Decimals ride the same accent — size + weight create the hierarchy, not a second hue.
  • One accent per surface. If you find yourself adding a second hue, the layout is wrong.
  • No gradients, no glows.
NUMBERS03

The reading is the hero

54.4°C
  • Integer: SemiBold, accent. Decimal: 56% size, accent. Unit: 30%, ink-2, baseline-aligned to integer top.
  • Tabular figures (font-feature-settings:'tnum') so columns line up.
  • Always a delta line: +1.4°C above prev. record. No delta → no post.
  • Always a station & UTC timestamp. Always a source.
VOICE04

Reporter, not pundit

DO
"Furnace Creek, CA — 54.4°C. +0.4 above 1913. Source: NOAA, 06:18 UTC."
DON'T
"🔥 UNREAL HEAT just shattered records in Death Valley. The planet is on fire…"
  • Place name first. Reading second. Delta third. Source fourth. Stop.
  • No emoji. No exclamation. No "shattered," "crushed," "wildfire."
  • Astonishment comes from the number, not the adjective.

SOURCE LINE06

Receipts, every time

SOURCE · NOAA · GHCN-D · 2026‑05‑06 18:42 UTC · CONF=HIGH

  • SOURCE · agency · dataset · ISO timestamp UTC · CONF=HIGH | MED | LOW.
  • If confidence is LOW, say so on the face of the post.
LAYOUT07

Editorial layout

  • Masthead: lockup + tagline. Nothing else on chrome.
  • 1px ink rule under masthead. No drop-shadows.
  • One reading per card. If you have two, make two cards.
  • Generous paper. Min 48px margins on banners; 64px on OG.
WHEN TO POST08

The bar is “extraordinary”

  • Post: all-time station record · multi-decade margin · physically improbable reading (e.g. Verkhoyansk 38°C in May).
  • Post with care: monthly record · regional record · streak end. Tag context.
  • Don't post: "warm day." We are not a weather feed.
  • Cadence: 2–6 posts/week. Silence is on-brand.
CHECKLIST09

Before you hit send

  • ☐ Place name is the headline.
  • ☐ Reading uses integer/decimal/unit hierarchy.
  • ☐ Delta line is present and signed (+ or −).
  • ☐ Source, dataset, UTC timestamp, confidence.
  • ☐ One accent. No emoji. No exclamation point.
  • ☐ If you removed the delta, the post dies.
