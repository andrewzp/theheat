## Root cause (`theheat-bot`)

Failing test: `tests/test_scheduler.py::test_breaker_records_skipped_not_failed`
— `assert bot_state["source_health"]["slow_source"]["failed"] == 3` got `0`.

A genuine **frozen-date time bomb**, confirmed by the runs being non-monotonic
(fail 11:16Z, success 13:21Z, fail 14:19Z) on the *identical* commit `6681880`:

- `record_source_health` keeps a rolling 7-day summary; the window cutoff is
  `latest_run_ts − 7d` (`_source_health_window_cutoff` in `src/state.py`).
- The test seeded three `failed` records hardcoded at `2026-06-12`, then fired
  the breaker. The breaker's `skipped` record is written with a `now()`
  timestamp (`_record_source_run` passes no `timestamp`), which becomes the
  latest run and moves the cutoff to `2026-06-14`.
- Once wall-clock time passed `2026-06-19`, the `06-12` failures fell before the
  cutoff and were pruned → `failed == 0`.

Production is unaffected: real timeouts and the subsequent breaker skip all
happen within minutes, so they share a window and nothing prunes. The bug was
purely the test's stale fixture dates.
