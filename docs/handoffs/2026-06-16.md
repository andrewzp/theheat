## Design problem: claim & warrant model (2026-06-16)

Reviewing the live queue surfaced three `precipitation_extreme` drafts asserting
records that don't exist: Barrow "127.5 mm above the previous 7-day record of 300.0
mm" (300.0 is a hardcoded detection threshold, not a record), a "previous daily
record was 0.0 mm" (a cold/empty baseline), and 427.5 mm/week at Barrow (~4x its
annual precip — physically impossible). They passed safety, fact-check, and critic;
only a human caught them.

A five-whys trace lands on an architectural root, NOT a precip bug: **the system has
no model of a *claim* or its *warrant*.** Every source's untyped event dataclass can
represent illegal states (a record with no baseline), and every integrity gate
validates internal consistency (tweet vs bundle) rather than warrant or plausibility
— the StoryBundle is trusted as ground truth, so a detector bug becomes a
publishable falsehood. This is a software DESIGN problem, queued as its own
design-first workstream (no code until reviewed). It reshapes Phase D and evolves
the evidence contract from a completeness checker into a claim/provenance model.
